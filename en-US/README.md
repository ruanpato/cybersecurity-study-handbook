# Cybersecurity Handbook & Study Roadmap (en-US)

🌐 **Language:** **English (en-US)** · [Português (pt-BR)](../pt-BR/README.md) · [Home / Landing](../README.md)

> **Status: beta (v0.2).** An **introductory** book and progressive study roadmap for people
> starting a Computer Science degree who intend to build a long-term career in cybersecurity.
> Intermediate depth is **a work in progress**, declared openly.

This repository is, at once, an **introductory technical book**, a **phased study roadmap**, a
**catalog of hands-on labs and projects**, and a **map of specializations**. It is written to
be read on its own and also exported to PDF. Intermediate-level deepening of some chapters is
ongoing work, declared openly.

## Ethical and legal notice (read this first)

All offensive content described here is intended **exclusively** for:

- systems and machines you own;
- isolated lab environments built for training;
- educational platforms that **explicitly authorize** testing (CTFs, ranges);
- environments for which you hold **formal, written authorization**.

Testing, scanning, or exploiting third-party systems without authorization is a **crime** in
most jurisdictions (in Brazil, see Law 12.737/2012 and the Internet Civil Framework, Law
12.965/2014; more in [HANDBOOK.md](HANDBOOK.md#chapter-0)). This material does **not** teach
how to break into third parties, steal credentials, build functional malware, or run real
phishing campaigns. Offensive knowledge is always paired with its defensive counterpart: how to
detect, prevent, and reproduce safely.

You are fully responsible for how you use this material.

## How to study with this material

1. **Read actively.** Running commands is not enough. For each concept, be able to answer:
   what it is, why it exists, how it works, how it fails, and how it relates to security.
2. **Build the lab first.** See [HANDBOOK Chapter 1](HANDBOOK.md#chapter-1) and the
   [fundamentals lab](reader-labs/fundamentals/).
3. **Document everything.** Each lab becomes portfolio evidence. Use
   [LAB-TEMPLATE.md](reader-labs/LAB-TEMPLATE.md).
4. **Assess yourself honestly.** Use [PROGRESS-CHECKLIST.md](PROGRESS-CHECKLIST.md) monthly.
   Mastery requires evidence, not a subjective feeling.
5. **Review in cycles.** The roadmap tells you when to revisit earlier material.

## Realistic expectations

Nobody becomes a technical reference by finishing a few months of courses. This material
distinguishes six stages of growth — from introductory knowledge to recognition as a reference
— and makes clear that the journey to deep specialization usually takes **years** of study,
deliberate practice, research, writing, and community participation. See the full discussion in
the [HANDBOOK introduction](HANDBOOK.md#about-this-book).
